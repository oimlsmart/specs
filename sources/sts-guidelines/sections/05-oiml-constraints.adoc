[[_oiml_constraints]]
== Profile requirements over base NISO STS

=== General

The profile specifies additional normative requirements over base NISO STS 1.2.

=== Reused content shall carry `<permission>`

Any content reused by OIML from a source outside the OIML publication shall
carry a `<permission>` element identifying the copyright holder and the
license under which the content is reused.

*Why:* OIML's editorial policy requires that readers can identify the
provenance and license of every reused passage. NISO STS permits but does not
require `<permission>`; OIML makes it mandatory for reused content.

[NOTE]
====
"Reused content" includes quotations, figures, tables, code samples, and any
other material that OIML did not author. Content authored by OIML does not
require `<permission>`.
====

=== `<self-uri>` shall not occur inside `<self-xref>`

The `<self-uri>` element shall not occur as a descendant of `<self-xref>`.
The `@rid` attribute of `<self-xref>` is the only mechanism OIML permits for
self-references inside an OIML publication.

*Why:* OIML's rendering pipeline uses `@rid` to resolve internal cross
references at parse time; permitting `<self-uri>` inside `<self-xref>`
introduces two resolution paths and causes ambiguous link targets.

=== Every `<std>` shall carry `<title>` and a dated designation

Every `<std>` element shall contain a `<title>`, and every `<std-ref>` within
it shall declare datedness via `@type` (`dated` when the designation embeds
the publication year, e.g. `ISO 15223-1:2012`; `undated` otherwise). The
publication year rides inside the dated `<std-ref>` designation — the base
tag suite does not permit `<pub-date>` inside `<std>`.

*Why:* OIML bibliographic tooling renders citations by concatenating
`<std-ref>` and `<title>`; omitting either the title or the datedness marker
produces a malformed citation.
